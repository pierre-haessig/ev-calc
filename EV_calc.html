Electric vs Thermal vehicle calculator

About this calculator

With this interactive calculator, you can compare the “greenness” (ecological merit) of an electric vehicle (EV) compared to a regular car motored by an internal combustion engine (ICE). This ecological comparison is done solely in terms of CO2 emissions.

The general idea is that an EV requires more energy than an ICE car for its manufacturing because of the battery. The EV has thus a “CO2 debt” that can be repaid after traveling some distance if, as one may expect, the EV indeed emits less CO2 during usage (CO2 per unit distance). The computation of this distance to CO2 parity is the objective of this calculator.

This distance depends on several inputs which value is difficult to know precisely. [link to D. Ernst article here] Therefore, this calculator allows you to input lower and upper bounds for uncertain inputs and then propagate this uncertainty down to the final result. Playing a bit with the numbers, in particular the CO2 emissions of electricity generation, you may soon realize that there is no clear winner!

My conclusion on this however is clear: vehicles should be light and small. Indeed, with a small battery, EVs are definetely better than combustion-powered cars. My e-bike has only a 300 Wh (0.3 kWh) battery and consumes 6—11 Wh/km.
